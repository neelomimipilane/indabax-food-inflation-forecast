# Human Capital Analysis

## Executive Summary

The repository contains two Botswana-specific indicators that are available in the supplied human-capital dataset: FAO_CP_23012 and FAO_CP_23013. Both are price-index proxies rather than direct education, nutrition, or health measures, so the analysis is exploratory and should be read as a macroeconomic association exercise.

## Data and Methods

- The analysis uses the Botswana human-capital series from data/raw/05_human_capital_project.csv.
- Two indicators were retained for analysis: FAO_CP_23012 and FAO_CP_23013.
- The indicators were merged with the engineered food-inflation series from the preprocessing pipeline.
- Simple OLS regressions were fitted with food inflation as the explanatory variable and each indicator as the response variable.

## Statistical Results

| Indicator | Coefficient | p-value | R² | Interpretation |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| FAO_CP_23012 | -1.0540 | 0.0028 | 0.032 | statistically meaningful |
| FAO_CP_23013 | -0.8986 | 0.0109 | 0.023 | statistically meaningful |

## Projection Using the Submission Forecast

Projected values for April-September 2025 were generated by applying the fitted coefficients to the official submission forecast in outputs/predictions.csv.

## Interpretation

The estimated coefficients indicate how the available indicator proxies move with food inflation. The results are useful for framing the welfare implications of inflation shocks, but they are not causal estimates because the underlying dataset is limited to proxy indicators.

## Limitations

The supplied human-capital dataset does not contain direct education, nutrition, employment, or poverty measures. The analysis therefore uses the closest available price-index proxies and reports only the associations that can be supported by the repository data.
